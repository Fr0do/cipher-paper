\paragraph{Key visibility} varies font size (13\%$\to$7\% of frame height),
alpha (255$\to$200), and position randomization (centered$\to$random).
The key is always rendered in bright yellow (RGB 255,255,0) with a black drop shadow;
distractors use non-yellow colors (cyan, green, magenta) and are always smaller and dimmer.

\paragraph{NL ambiguity levels:}
(1)~exact canonical phrasing;
(2)~paraphrase from a pool of 4--6 templates per operation;
(3)~compositional with ``undo'' framing and embedded temporal cues.
All phrases are designed to be parseable by regex for S1 baseline scoring.

\paragraph{Operation Vocabulary.}
The operational vocabulary consists of six composable transformations: \texttt{reverse}, \texttt{hflip}, \texttt{vflip}, \texttt{rotate90}, \texttt{rotate270}, and \texttt{speed2x}. These operations are visually distinct, non-destructive, and strictly composable via standard \texttt{ffmpeg} filters. They demand different types of reasoning: spatial (\texttt{hflip}, \texttt{vflip}, \texttt{rotate}), and temporal (\texttt{reverse}, \texttt{speed2x}), providing a comprehensive testbed for multimodal execution without requiring complex parameter tuning from the model.

\subsection{Procedural generation and Dataset Integrity}

Samples are fully deterministic from $(seed, level)$.
Background clips are drawn uniformly from a Kinetics-400 pool
(diverse human activities, minimal on-screen text).
Test split: seeds 0--49. Challenge split: seeds 50--99.

A persistent challenge in evaluating large language and vision models is the risk of data contamination, where static benchmark data inadvertently leaks into the pre-training corpus. CIPHER mitigates this risk entirely through programmatic dataset synthesis. The benchmark is procedurally generated from a tuple of $(seed, \text{level})$, which deterministically selects the background clip, the randomly generated 7-character key, the sequence of operations, and the natural language instruction template. Because samples are dynamically constructed on-demand using disjoint background video pools, researchers can regenerate the benchmark with novel seeds, guaranteeing an uncontaminated evaluation environment and an effectively infinite supply of test instances.

\subsection{Difficulty Calibration via GEPA}
\label{sec:gepa}

A benchmark that saturates (100\% EM) or collapses (0\% EM) across all models conveys
no gradient. To locate a generator configuration that produces partial credit for
frontier VLMs, we adopt a GEPA-style \citep{gepa} reflective evolutionary loop over
the image-mode generator. The generator is specified as an S-expression DSL over
five tunable numerical parameters: key alpha $\alpha$, font size as a fraction of
frame height, number and chromatic composition of distractors, Gaussian noise
$\sigma$, and Gaussian blur radius. Each \emph{program} corresponds to a fixed
instantiation of these parameters; \emph{fitness} is defined as
$-|\text{EM} - 0.03| - 0.5 \cdot \text{miss rate}$, targeting a 3\% sweet spot at
which models read but fail on individual characters.

We evaluated four rounds of five programs each, with four samples per program
(eight in the last round), using Gemini Flash as the calibration model. Round~1
performed a wide parameter sweep; subsequent rounds progressively narrowed the
region around the fitness peak. Round~3 produced a program
($\alpha{=}145$, font $4.8\%$, three distractors with two yellow, noise
$\sigma{=}4$, blur $0.25$) yielding 0\% EM with predictions of the correct
length—a \emph{near-miss} regime where characters such as
\texttt{Q}/\texttt{O}, \texttt{8}/\texttt{B}, \texttt{X}/\texttt{K},
\texttt{6}/\texttt{G} are consistently confused. This regime is what we use as
the default L3--L5 extraction condition: it is solvable but not trivial for
Gemini-class readers, while remaining entirely uninformative (0\% EM) for non-VLM
baselines.

\subsection{Metrics}
\label{sec:metrics}

\paragraph{EM.} $\frac{1}{N}\sum_i \mathbf{1}[\hat{k}_i = k^*_i]$.

\paragraph{CharF1.} Character-level precision/recall F1 (partial credit).

\paragraph{PipeF1.}
\begin{equation}
\text{PipeF1} = 0.25\cdot\overline{\text{S1}} + 0.25\cdot\overline{\text{S2}} + 0.50\cdot\overline{\text{CharF1}}
\end{equation}
S3 receives double weight as the terminal objective;
S1/S2 serve as diagnostic leading indicators.
